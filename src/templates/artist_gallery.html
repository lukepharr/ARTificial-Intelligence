Artist's Gallery: Evolution

Throughout an artist's lifetime, changes in method, subject matter, and even style are to be expected. This is certainly true for the artists we've selected for this project. Both Rothko and Morris have careers that span more than 30 years with several distinct periods of work summarized below.

Rothko's Periods of Work

Figurative (1936-1939)

Modestly sized oil paintings on canvas depicting fuzzy figures and landscapes

Surrealism (1940-1946)

Rothko works in small watercolors and large canvases during this period. His subjects draw from mythology and do not always have obvious literal interpretations.

Multiform (1947-1949)

Rothko is now fully immersed in abstraction. His paintings are not yet geometrical rarified, though they contain a lot of the visual vernacular of his mature period.

Mature (1950-1967)

othko has now arrived at his signature style. Rectangles float on the canvas composed of dozens of thin washes of diluted oil paint stacked one on top of the next.

Late (1968-1970)

These are the works between Rothko’s aneurism and his eventual suicide. They are largely pieces on paper done with acrylics, and sometimes oil. The rectangles borders begin to merge with the edge of the painting, and grey, brown and black become central colors—though there are also many bright blue, red and pink works from this period.

Louis's Periods of Work

Early Paintings (1934-53)

Early works which are both abstract and figurative.

Veil Paintings (1954)

This period represents Louis’s first experiments with the repetitive pouring technique he used to create veils of color.

Abstract Expressionist (1955-1957)

This period represents Morris Louis’s brief stint in the New York school of Abstract Expressionists exemplified by such artists as de Kooning and Pollock.

Veil paintings (1958-59)

ouis returns to the veil technique he had abandoned in favor of abstract expressionism. During these two years, Louis produced hundreds of monumental-scale paintings.

Themes and Variations (1959-60)

A reworking of the veil paintings with creative twists. Seal, 1959 epitomizes this.

Unfurled Paintings (1960-61)

Louis began working with stripes in this period.

Stripe Paintings (1961-62)

In his last period, Louis worked with simple draw down patterns, produced columns of nested colors streaking across the canvas at simple angles. This was his most prolific period.
